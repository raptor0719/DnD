Downtime Activities

Healing potions can be extremely useful to a party to be healthy in a pinch.

Time
Time dependinng on power of potion
Resources
Gold depending on power of potion
Requirements
Proficiency of an herbalism kit and access to one, or an equivelant setup

Buying a Magic Item

Purchasing a magic item requires time and money to seek out and contact people willing to sell items. Even then, there is no guarantee a seller will have the items a character desires.

Time
At least 1 workweek
Resources
100gp
Roll
Charisma (Persuasion)
Bonus
+1 to roll per additional Time/Resources spent up to a maximum of 10
Result
A potential magic item to purchase.
Carousing
Time
1 workweek
Resources
10gp, 50gp, or 250gp for lower, middle, and upper class respectively
Roll
Charisma (Persuasion)
Result
Possibility of Hostile or Allied contacts. A hostile contact works against the player, whereas an allied contact will aid the player. A character can only have (1 + CHA MOD) allied contacts, with a minimum of 1, at a time.
Crafting an Item

A characer who has the time, the money, and the needed tools can use downtime to craft armor, weapons, clothing, or other kinds of gear.

Time
(Gp cost of item)/50 workweeks
Resources
Appropriate tool and (Gp cost of item)/2 worth of materials
Bonus
Multiple characters can combine effort to create an item. Time = Workweeks/Characters.
Result
An item!
Crime

Sometime it pays to be bad. This activity gives a character the chance to make some extra cash, at the risk of arrest.

Time
1 week
Resources
25gp to gather information on potential targets
Roll
The character chooses a DC for the target reward based on the table below. The rolls are the following: Dexterity (Stealth), Dexterity using Thieves' Tools, and the choice of Intelligence (Investigation), Wisdom (Perception), or Charisma (Deception).
Result
If none of the checks are successful, the character is jailed. They must pay a fine equal to potential earning and spend 1 week in jail per 25gp of the fine. If one check succeeds, the heist fails but the character escapes. Two checks successful, 1/2 the payout. Three checks successful, the full payout.
Payouts
DC Payout
10 50gp
15 100gp
20 200gp
25 1000gp
Gambling

Games of chance are a way to make a fortune - and perhaps a better way to lose one.

Time
1 workweek
Resources
At least 10gp
Roll
3 separate checks, Wisdom (Insight), Charisma (Deception), and Charisma (Intimidation). The DC for each check is determined by the DM.
Result`
The results below assume you've already paid your initial wager. For example: Jerry wagers 10gp and rolls 1 success. So, the result is -10gp + 0.5 * 10gp = -10gp + 5gp = -5gp. Jerry lost 5gp from gambling.
Payout
Successes Payout
0 Lost 1*Wager
1 Gain 0.5*Wager
2 Gain 1.5*Wager
3 Gain 2*Wager
Pit Fighting

Pit fighting includes boxing, wrestling, and other non lethal forms of combat in an organized setting with predetermined matches.

Time
1 workweek
Roll
3 separate checks, Strength (Athletics), Dexterity (Acrobatics), and a special constitution check which is 1d20 + CON bonus + hit die roll. The character can also replace one of these checks with an attack roll with a weapon of choice that is valid for the type of combat. The DC for each check is determined by the DM.
Result
Successes Reward
0 None
1 50gp
2 100gp
3 200gp
Relaxation

Sometimes the best thing to do between adventures is relax. Whether a character wants a hard earned vacation or needs to recover from injuries, relaxation is the ideal option for adventurers who need a break.

Time
1 week
Resources
A modest lifestyle for the whole week (1 gp for each day = 7 gp)
Result
  • During the action, gain advantage on saving throws vs long-acting disease/poisons
  • At the end of the rest can do EITHER (only non-magical effects):
    • End one effect that keeps the character from regaining hitpoints
    • Can restore one ability score that has been reduced
Religious Service

Characters with a religious bent might want to spend downtime in service to a temple, either by attending rites or by proselytizing in the community.

Time
1 workweek
Roll
1 of either Intelligence (Religion) or Charisma (Persuasion)
Result
Possibility of 1 or more favors from the clergy or god of the religion. The favor can be expended to request aid such as political or social support, help with dealing with a specific problem, knowledge, deity intervention such as an omen or vision (up to DM's discretion). A character may only have (1 + CHA MOD) favors, with a minimum of 1, at a time.
Research

Forewarned is forearmed. The research downtime activity allows a character to delve into lore concerning a monster, a location, a magic item, or some other particular topic.

Time
1 workweek
Resources
Access to local sage or library and 50gp spent on bribes, materials, gifts, etc
Roll
Intelligence check
Bonus
+1 per additional 100gp spent beyond first to a max of 6. Advantage if the sage or library is particularly well stocked or suited toward the research project.
Result
Possibility of learning 1-3 pieces of lore about the chosen topic.
Scribing a Spell Scroll

With time and patience, a spellcaster can transfer a spell to a scroll, creating a spell scroll. They may also apply a magical cipher, making the scroll more difficult to read.

Time
Time corresponding to the spell level (shown in chart below)
Resources
Gold corresponding to the spell level (shown in chart below) and the materials required to cast the spell
Requirements
  • Proficiency in the Arcana skill
  • The character must know the spell they are attempting to scribe

Selling a Magic Item

Selling a magic item is by no means an easy task. Con artists and thieves are always looking out for an easy score, and there's no guarantee that a character will receive a good offer even if a legitimate buyer is found.

Time
1 workweek
Resources
An item to sell and 25gp used to spread word of the desired sell
Roll
Charisma (Persuasion)
Result
A potential buyer for the magic item.
Training

Given enough free time and the services of an instructor, a character can learn a language or pick up a proficiency with a tool. This training does not have to occur consecutively, but could incur penalties if there is too much time between training sessions.

Time
(10 - INT MOD) workweeks, there is no penalty for a negative INT MOD.
Resources
25 gp per workweek
Result
Learn a new skill
Work

When all else fails, an adventurer can turn to an honest trade to earn a living, This activity represents a character's attempt to find temporary work, the quality and wages of which are difficult to predict.

Time
1 workweek
Roll
1 of STR (Athletics), DEX (Acrobatics), INT using tools, CHA (Performance), or CHA using an instrument
Result
A wage between 2sp and 2gp based on the roll.
